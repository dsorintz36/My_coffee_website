What is Coffee?

Coffee is a globally popular beverage brewed from roasted and ground seeds of the tropical evergreen coffee plant,
primarily Coffea arabica and Coffea canephora (robusta).
Known for its stimulating effect due to caffeine, coffee is one of the most consumed drinks worldwide,
alongside water and tea.

Origins & History üåé

Coffee's origins trace back to the Ethiopian highlands, where wild coffee plants are native.
The beverage's use began in 15th-century Arabia, particularly in Yemen, where it was consumed in Sufi shrines
to aid concentration during prayers.
From there, coffee spread to the Middle East, North Africa, Europe, and eventually worldwide.

COFFEE
AMERICANO

Is made by adding hot water to espresso, resulting in a smoother and less intense drink that is similar to black coffee

ESSPRESSO

Espresso is a small, concentrated shot of coffee with a bold and intense flavor. It is the foundation for many other coffee drinks.

CAPPUCCINO

Cappuccino is made with equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam, giving it a balanced, rich taste with a frothy top.

LATTE

Latte combines one shot of espresso with a large amount of steamed milk and a small layer of foam, creating a creamy and mild coffee.

MOCHA

Mocha blends espresso with chocolate and steamed milk, offering a sweet and rich coffee with a dessert-like quality.

MACCHIATO

Macchiato is an espresso with just a small amount of milk or milk foam added, giving it a strong flavor with a slight creamy touch.
